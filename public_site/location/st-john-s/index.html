Frequently asked questions

How do antique appraisals work in St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador?

Most antique appraisers in St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador review condition, age, maker marks, materials, and comparable sales to estimate fair market value. A written report is often used for insurance, estates, donations, or resale.

What should I prepare before contacting an appraiser?

Bring clear photos (front/back/details/marks), measurements, condition notes, and any provenance (receipts, family history, restoration notes).

How much does an antique appraisal cost in St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador?

Pricing varies by item type and scope (single item vs. estate). Many providers quote a flat fee or hourly rate; ask whether the fee includes a written report and research/comparables.
